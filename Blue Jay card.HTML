Blue Jay

Blue Jays aren't actually blue. Scattering light in the structural parts of the feathers causes the feathers to appear blue.

  • Scientific Name: Cyanocitta cristata
  • Average Length: 11 inches
  • Wingspan: 16 inches
  • Migration: Migratory

While originally a forest dweller, Blue Jays have adapted to cities, parks, gardens, and forest fragmentation. It is easily recognized by its large size; blue-purple upperparts, wings, and tail; and bluish purple crest.
